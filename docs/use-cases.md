# Use cases

Web MCP lets your AI assistant use a real browser. Here are ways you can put it to work.

## Browse the web

You can ask your AI to go to any website and get information from it.

Examples:
- "Find the latest news about space exploration"
- "Check the price of a product on Amazon"
- "Read the documentation for a library I want to use"

The AI opens a browser, goes to the site, reads the page, and tells you what it found.

## Fill out forms

Your AI can fill in text fields, select options from dropdowns, and submit forms.

Examples:
- "Fill out this contact form with my details"
- "Sign up for a newsletter on this site"
- "Submit a support ticket with this description"

## Test websites and apps

If you are building a website or web app, you can ask your AI to test parts of it.

Examples:
- "Go through the checkout flow and tell me if anything breaks"
- "Test the search feature and see if results show up correctly"
- "Check all the links on the homepage"

## Extract data

Your AI can read content from websites and organize it for you.

Examples:
- "Go to this list of job postings and make a table of the first 10 results"
- "Check the weather forecast for this week and summarize it"
- "Read the top stories on this news site and give me a summary"

## Monitor dashboards and accounts

You can ask your AI to check on things regularly.

Examples:
- "Log in to my analytics account and tell me the numbers for today"
- "Check my email inbox and tell me if there are any urgent messages"
- "Look at my order history and tell me when my last order was"

## Chain multiple actions together

You can combine several steps into one request.

Examples:
- "Go to google.com, search for best laptops under 1000 dollars, open the first result, and summarize what you find"
- "Log in to my project management tool, open the active sprint, and list all incomplete tasks"
- "Go to this recipe site, find a recipe for pasta, read the ingredients, and write them down for me"
